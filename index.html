About the Group

We seek principles for designing next-generation optoelectronics, heterogeneous catalysts, and quantum information technologies. To discover such design principles, we develop state-of-the-art dynamics methods, as well as accurate electronic structure and machine-learning representations of ground and excited state potential energy surfaces.

We aspire to be a world-leading research group, not only through transformative discoveries but also in fostering a supportive environment where every member grows individually and as part of the team.

Research Directions

We study the fascinating consequences of interactions between electronic excitations and atomic or lattice displacements in condensed-phase systems, often under out-of-equilibrium conditions. We develop numerical simulations to discover i) how such interactions determine spectroscopic and kinetic responses measured in experiments, and ii) how such interactions can be tailored for designing functional materials. We are currently invested in the following directions:

Electrochemical Interfaces

Solid-liquid and solid-solid interfaces govern modern energy conversion, yet their microscopic charge-transfer (CT) mechanisms remain fundamentally unresolved. We are building machine-learning approaches to enable fully atomistic CT dynamics simulations at electrochemical interfaces.

Magnetic Quantum Materials

Interaction between spins and lattice displacements critically determines the performance of candidate materials for quantum computing applications. We are developing quantum dynamics methods to resolve the signatures of coupled quasiparticle excitations in magnetic materials.

Condensed-phase Spectroscopy

Time-resolved spectroscopy of biological and solid-state materials reveals intriguing environmental effects that critically shape reaction pathways and finite-temperature response. We are formulating first-principles approaches to simulate electronic/vibrational spectroscopy in the ultrafast regime.

About Sohang

Sohang Kundu is an incoming Assistant Professor in the Department of Chemistry and Biochemistry at The Ohio State University. Sohang grew up in India, and completed his BSc. from Presidency University in 2015 and MSc. from IIT Bombay in 2017. He then moved to the US for graduate studies, and obtained his PhD in 2023 from the University of Illinois Urbana Champaign, working with Prof. Nancy Makri. From 2023-2026 he was a postdoctoral research scientist in Prof. Tim Berkelbach's group at Columbia University. Sohang will join the faculty at OSU in July, 2026.
